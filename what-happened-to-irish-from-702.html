What Happened To Irish From 702?

Posted by Billy Koelling on Thursday, August 8, 2024

Orish Grinstead, known as Irish, was a member of the R&B girl group 702, which gained popularity in the late 1990s and early 2000s. The group, named after the area code of their hometown of Las Vegas, Nevada, was composed of sisters Irish and LeMisha Grinstead, along with lead singer Kameelah Williams. They are best known for hits such as “Where My Girls At?” and “Get It Together.” However, as the music industry evolved and the group’s dynamic changed, Irish’s journey with 702 took several turns, leading fans to wonder about her whereabouts and activities post-702.

FAQ Section

What led to Irish’s departure from 702?

Irish left 702 due to a combination of internal group conflicts, the changing music industry, and a desire to pursue personal interests and possibly a solo career.

Has Irish released any solo music since leaving 702?

While Irish has explored solo projects, she has not achieved the same level of success as she did with 702. Information on her solo releases is limited.

Is there a possibility of a 702 reunion with Irish?

There have been rumors and one-off performances that suggest a 702 reunion could happen, but nothing has been confirmed regarding a permanent reunion with Irish as of the last known updates.

Conclusion

In conclusion, Irish Grinstead’s journey with 702 was marked by significant contributions to the R&B genre during the group’s peak. Despite the challenges and eventual disbandment of the group, Irish’s legacy as part of 702 remains influential. Her departure from the group led her down a path of personal exploration and growth. While she has maintained a lower profile compared to her days in the spotlight with 702, her fans continue to remember her contributions fondly. The possibility of a reunion, while uncertain, keeps the hope alive for those who cherish the era of 90s R&B that 702 so vividly represents.
