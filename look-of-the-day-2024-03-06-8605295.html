Sophie Turner Ditched Her Rigid Jeans for Comfy Party Pants

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, August 20, 2024

When I think of statement pieces, tops, shoes, and accessories come to mind — but pants have the potential to make just as big of an impact. Celebrities are on the same page opting for fun pants left and right; Katie Holmes has worn sequin trousers, Emily Ratajkowski rocked parachute pants, and Rihanna made maroon leather bottoms her own — and now, we can add Sophie Turner to the growing list. 

While attending the Louis Vuitton Fall/Winter Paris Fashion Week show, Turner wore an off-the-shoulder blouse featuring draped sleeves and a corset bodice accessorized with leather suspenders. Her Louis Vuitton black knot pumps coordinated with her striped, high-waisted bottoms that I’m officially dubbing “party pants.” As opposed to a more low-key choice, her pants commanded attention and became the focal point of her ensemble.  

Grace Karin High-Waist Striped Pants

Steve Madden Rumi High-Waist Striped Pants

Striped bottoms can seem daunting but a simple styling trick is all you need to master the exciting print. Match your new pants with a solid-colored top, whether it be a simple T-shirt or an elevated cowl-neck blouse. Apply the same tip when adding shoes, slipping on a staple black, beige, or neutral pair. 

Not only are striped bottoms simple to master, but they’re comfortable, too. Instead of tight, restrictive denim, trousers are often made of soft, breezy fabrics like cotton and linen. The flowy material lets you move with freedom, creating a sweatpants-like experience. Not to mention, the light textiles are for perfect spring, creating the ideal transitional pants for the season.      

Grace Karin offers a $38 lookalike pair that features a similar high waist and tapered pant legs. A removable tie-front belt adds a unique touch, while the cropped length compliments sandals and ballet flats beautifully. Steve Madden takes a chic business approach with its Rumi High-Rise Striped Pants, including a zipper and hook closure, spacious pockets, and full-length inseam. Both options work great for professional environments such as work, but also double as going-out pants thanks to their individual prints.   

Turner officially convinced me to trade in my jeans for striped pants, and if you’re in the same boat, then you’re going to want to shop the below picks, including loose, tie-front, and palazzo options.
