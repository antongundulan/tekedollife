What To Do In Denver In November

Posted by Patria Henriques on Tuesday, August 20, 2024
People are currently reading this guide.

Denver in November: Don't Be a Square, Explore the Mile High City!

So, you're thinking of visiting Denver in November? Hold on to your cowboy hats, folks, because this city is about to prove that fall ain't all about pumpkin spice lattes and chunky knit sweaters (although, hey, we won't judge). Denver in November is a wild west adventure with a dash of holiday cheer sprinkled on top.

Ditch the Crowds, Embrace the Crisp Air

Forget the summer throngs! November offers a sweet spot where the weather is cool and crisp, perfect for exploring without feeling like you're wading through a sea of tourists. Plus, with the leaves starting to turn, Denver transforms into a kaleidoscope of fiery reds and golden yellows.

Pro Tip: Don't forget to pack layers! The weather in November can be a bit of a fickle friend, one minute basking in sunshine, the next threatening a snowball fight.

Hitting the High Notes: Outdoor Adventures Await

Denver boasts some of the most epic scenery in the country, and November is a perfect time to snag a glimpse.

  • **Rocky Mountain National Park: **Take a scenic drive and witness the mountains cloaked in their autumnal best. Feeling adventurous? Lace up your hiking boots and conquer a trail (just be sure to check the conditions beforehand â€“ snowmageddon might have other plans).

  • Urban Exploration: Denver itself is an outdoor enthusiast's paradise. Rent a bike and cruise through the city or take a walk along the South Platte River. Breathe in that fresh mountain air and pretend you're a rugged explorer (with way better snacks).

Don't Hibernate Just Yet: Indoor Fun for the Frigid Soul

Let's be honest, sometimes the mountains just aren't calling your name (especially if they're covered in a foot of snow). But fear not, Denver has plenty to offer the indoor adventurer as well.

  • Museum Mania: Denver's got museums to rival any big city. Dive into the Denver Art Museum's vast collection or explore the wonders of the natural world at the Denver Museum of Nature and Science.

  • Craft Beer Bonanza: Denver is a haven for craft beer lovers. Take a brewery tour and sample some of the city's finest suds, or simply cozy up in a pub and savor a pint (or two).

  • Holiday Happenings: As November ushers in the holiday season, Denver transforms into a winter wonderland. Catch the holiday lights at the Denver Botanic Gardens or hop on the Polar Express Train Ride for a dose of festive cheer.

FAQs:

How to pack for Denver in November?

Layers are your friend! Pack for sunshine, chilly mornings, and the occasional snow flurry.

How to get around Denver?

Denver has a decent public transportation system, but renting a car gives you the flexibility to explore the surrounding areas.

How to find the best craft breweries?

Ask any local! Denverites are passionate about their beer scene. You can also find brewery tours online or through apps.

How to score tickets to the Polar Express Train Ride?

Tickets tend to sell out fast, so book well in advance! Check the Colorado Railroad Museum website for details.

How to avoid the crowds?

Weekends tend to be busier, so plan your outdoor adventures for weekdays.
