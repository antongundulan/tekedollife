Randall Cobb Biography, Married, Wife, Girlfriend, Parents, Height, Weight

Posted by Kelle Repass on Wednesday, August 28, 2024

Professional American football player RandallCobb joined the Green Bay Packers of the National Football League after the NFL Draft in 2011. Before turning professional, the talented talented player had a distinguished career in college football at the University of Kentucky , becoming one of the greatest players in Kentucky Wildcats history.

Age, early childhood and rise to fame

Born August 22, 1990, Randall Ladonald Cobb II attended high school at Alcoa High School in Alcoa, Tennessee. As a tri-sport athlete, Cobb was entered into football, basketball, and track. In his junior year he was named All District Basketball Player and in his senior year he earned a 3rd place finish in the 100 meters at the state meet and also helped the 4× relay team 100 m from the school to a first second place.

During his high school days, Randall Cobb played football in the offensive and defensive positions but was mostly dominant as a quarterback. The school spent 4 consecutive years as the Class AA State Champion with Cobb winning the Tennessee Secondary School Athletic Association’s Mr. Football Award.

Cobb had a college education at the University of Kentucky where he played for the Kentucky Wildcats football team. Playing at quarterback, he appeared in 11 games as a freshman, recording 11 touchdowns, a performance that earned him SEC All-Freshmen team status. He moved to wide receiver in his second season in which he had 15 touchdowns – 4 receptions, 1 return and 10 rushes.

The talented player finished his junior season (2010) with 1,017 receiving yards, 424 rushing yards, 58 yards with a total of 15 touchdowns – 7 receptions, 5 rushing and 3 assists. He also set a new SEC single-season record for net yardage with 2,396. His outstanding performance earned him first-team All-American designations as well as a consensus all-team SEC selection.

At the time of leaving the Kentucky Wildcats, Randall Cobb was the school’s all-time leader in touchdowns (37). Dropping his college year of college football, Cobb was declared a candidate for the 2011 NFL Draft on January 14, 2011. He was selected in the second round by the Green Bay Packers as the 64th pick in the draft.

NFL Career

Randall Cobb made his NFL debut on September 8. In 2011, during the Packers’ game against the New Orleans Saints, he became the first player born in the 90s to play in the NFL. He finished his rookie season with 25 passes for 375 yards and a touchdown. Cobb recorded an even more impressive showing on special teams as he tallied 34 shots on goal for 941 yards and a touchdown, 26 punt returns for 295 yards and a touchdown.

In his 2012 season, Cobb started 8 games in total, leading the team in 15 appearances in receptions (80) and receptions (954), in addition to an 8th-best second on the team. He created a Packers franchise single-season record of 2,342 all-purpose yards – 954 receiving, 132 rushing, 964 kickoff returns, and 292 kickoff returns, including a touchdown. Cobb’s 2013 season was marked by an injury in the Week 6 game against the Baltimore Raven, after which he finished the season with 31 receptions for 433 receiving yards and 4 touchdowns.

Randall Cobb returned to form in the 2014 season which he finished with 91 receptions on 126 targets for 1,287 yards and 12 touchdowns. His performance earned him a selection for the 2014 Pro Bowl. For the 2015 season, the talented player recorded a career-best 79 receptions on 129 targets, for 829 yards and 6 touchdowns. He finished the 2016 season with 60 receptions on 84 targets for 610 yards and 4 touchdowns. In 2017, Randall recorded 66 receptions for 653 yards and 4 touchdowns.

Wife or girlfriend, is Randall Cobb married?

Aiyda Cobb (née Ghahramani), wife of Randall Cobb is a Washington DC-based lawyer. Before attending the University of Hampshire Law School, Aiyda Ghahramani studied at Rutgers State University, where she practiced athletics.

The couple reportedly met in 2011 at an EA Sports event held at the Lavo hotspot in Manhattan. According to Aiyda Ghahramani, they had an instant connection and coincidentally Cobb only had a few days to get drafted into the 2011 draft. They remained just friends for 3 years until 2014 when their relationship became exclusive.

After dating for 3 years, the duo made yet another transition as they tied the knot on April 12, 2017 in a lavish ceremony held at The Pierre in New York City. On February 20, 2018, the NFL star announced that she was expecting her first child, a son due in August 2018.

His parents

Randall Cobb was born in Maryville, Tennessee to Tina Cobb and Randall Cobb Sr. His mother was a track athlete and after graduation she worked at an auto parts factory where she met Cobb’s father.

In addition to his own work at the factory, Randall Cobb Sr. worked many times to support the family. Growing up, Cobb’s family moved around a lot, living in 7 different homes before the youngster was 14. He was raised alongside a sibling, his older brother, Michael Love.

Height, weight and body measurements

Randall Cobb stands at a height of 5 feet 10 (178 cm) and maintains a body weight of 87 kg (192 lbs). Along with his innate talents, Cobb’s unparalleled running ability makes him a dominant force on the Packers’ offense.
