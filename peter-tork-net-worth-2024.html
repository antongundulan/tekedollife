Early Life and Career Beginnings

Peter Tork was born in Washington, D.C., and showed an early interest in music. He learned to play several instruments, including the piano, banjo, and acoustic guitar. His passion for music led him to Greenwich Village, New York, where he became part of the folk music scene in the early 1960s.

Joining The Monkees

In 1965, Tork was chosen as a member of The Monkees, a band created for a television show of the same name. The show was a massive success, and so was the band, with several of their albums reaching the top of the charts.
