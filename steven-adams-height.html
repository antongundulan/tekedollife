How tall is Steven Adams?

Posted by Kelle Repass on Wednesday, August 7, 2024

Steven Adams entered the NBA as only the third Kiwi-born Basketball player through the 2013 NBA Draft and Joined Oklahoma City Thunder (previously Seattle Supersonics). He began showing signs of prowess from his very first season and it worked to initiate conversations about his potential and longevity in one of basketball’s most renowned tournaments. Steven’s success thus far can be attributed to his ability to utilize his Physical Attributes and make the most of his talents.

Steven Adams Height

Steve’s height measures 6 feet 11 inches in length, making him a Center. Regardless of their height, a Center has to be an agile contributor for his team, if not by points, then by other means.

In Steven’s case, a near-seven feet stature gives him the advantage of being a post-up player and perimeter shooter on top of being a defensive asset for his team.

Steven Adams Other Physical Attributes

Steven Adams' combination of physical attributes collectively contribute to making him a difference-maker on the court, let us see how:

Steven’s Wingspan

Steven’s Wingspan measures an impressive 7 feet 5 inches wide. The importance of long arms for Steven can be understood by the fact that he maintains a career average of 8.2 rebounds, 0.9 steals, and one block. Such elongated arms enable him to disrupt passing lanes and excel in contesting shots near or away from the basket.

Those long arms also give Steven extra stability and control when handling the ball or shooting from a distance. Despite not being a three-point shooter, he still maintains excellent accuracy.

Steven’s Weight

Steven Adam weighs an imposing 265 pounds (120 kg). Understanding Steven Adams' physicality lies within his weight. An athlete's weight plays a pivotal role in their ability to maintain balance and withstand contact during intense gameplay situations.

Where Point guards are required to maintain anywhere between 180 to 210 pounds to be effective passers and scorers, Centers like Steven Adams are required to be heavier, more muscular, and physically challenging to be successful at defending the rim since a lot of the play takes place surrounding the board.

Steven’s Shoe Size

Lastly, we delve into the size of the shoes that Steve wears. The sizable base supporting his feet comes equipped with footwear measuring up to size 18 US/17.5 UK/55 Euro – truly remarkable dimensions reflecting Adams' unique physique.

Not many might know, but Shoes worn by Power forwards and Centers serve slightly different purposes than those worn by guards.

Shoes worn by Point guards and Shooting guards are designed to be more flexible to support the quickness of their feet, whereas shoes worn by Power forwards and Centers are created to provide stability as taller players are more prone to foot injuries.

Overall, As Steven Adams continues to showcase his skills and physical prowess on the court, he continues to make his case as one of the most influential Kiwis in Basketball history. When the time comes for Steven to hang his shoes, he will leave active competition as one of the best players to have played the game.
