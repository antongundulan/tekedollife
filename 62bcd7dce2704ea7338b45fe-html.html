Boxing: Julio Csar Chvez net worth: the fortune of the Mexican champion

Posted by Martina Birk on Sunday, August 25, 2024

Julio César Chávez is a former professional boxer widely considered to be the best Mexican fighter of all time, as well as one of the best boxers in the history of the world. He currently works as a sports commentator.

Born on July 12, 1962 in Ciudad Obregón, Sonora, he won six world championships in three different weight divisions during his 25-year career in the ring.

His titles

In 1984, Chavez Gonzalez won the WBC super featherweight title, three years later he won the WBA lightweight title.

In 1988 he won the WBC lightweight title and a year later he won the WBC light welterweight title.

In 1990, Julio Cesar won the IBF light welterweight title. And four years later he won the WBC light welterweight title.

Holder of several records

The greatest boxer in Mexican history holds the record for most title fights at 37, most title fights won at 31 and most successful title defenses at 27.

He also has the longest undefeated streak in boxing history: lasting 13 years.

His bout against Greg Haugen, held at the Azteca Stadium, set a record for the largest attendance at a boxing match with more than 132,000 people.

Julio César Chávez was inducted into the International Boxing Hall of Fame in 2011.

Currently, the "Great Mexican Champion" is a sports commentator for TV Azteca, on Box Azteca shows.

How much is his fortune?

According to Celebrity Net Worth, Julio César Chávez has a net worth of 10 million dollars.
